The Birth of Bitcoin

In 2008, a mysterious figure named Satoshi Nakamoto released the Bitcoin whitepaper, proposing a decentralized peer-to-peer digital currency. In January 2009, the first block, known as the Genesis Block, was mined. Since then, Bitcoin has grown from a niche experiment to a multi-trillion-dollar ecosystem.

How It Works

Bitcoin uses a decentralized ledger called the blockchain. Transactions are verified by miners using cryptographic proof-of-work, making it nearly impossible to alter or fake data. New bitcoins are minted through a process called mining, rewarding those who secure the network.

Why Bitcoin Matters

Bitcoin empowers people in countries with unstable currencies or authoritarian governments. It allows anyone with internet access to store and transfer value globally, without censorship or middlemen. It also offers a hedge against inflation and traditional fiat devaluation.
